Operating artifact

The Delivery Weave makes integration testable.

Three technical threads pass through five decision gates. Each gate makes a different assumption visible before it becomes rework.

01

Ground the work before architecture hardens.

Decision

What workflow outcome matters, what data boundary applies, and where must human authority remain?

Failure mode

A technically impressive prototype with unclear ownership, user behavior, evidence, or acceptance criteria.

Proof

Baseline, workflow map, data contract, risk assumptions, and an evaluation seed set.

02

Compose the smallest coherent system.

Decision

Which responsibilities belong in retrieval, tools, orchestration, deterministic services, UI, and human review?

Failure mode

One giant prompt absorbs business rules, security boundaries, and integration logic that should be explicit.

Proof

Interface contracts, bounded permissions, fallback paths, architecture decisions, and cost assumptions.

03

Prove behavior, not demo charisma.

Decision

What does acceptable mean for task success, groundedness, safety, privacy, latency, and cost?

Failure mode

Stakeholders approve the demo while edge cases, hallucination paths, and escalation failures remain invisible.

Proof

Golden cases, adversarial cases, human review criteria, failure taxonomy, and release thresholds.

04

Observe the system inside the work.

Decision

Which signals separate usage from value and model issues from workflow, data, or integration issues?

Failure mode

Teams count calls and tokens while users route around the system or quality degradation goes unnoticed.

Proof

Outcome metrics, adoption depth, overrides, escalations, defects, latency, and unit economics.

05

Harvest what is reusable without flattening context.

Decision

What should become a component, accelerator, framework, playbook, evaluation asset, or cautionary pattern?

Failure mode

Teams either rebuild everything or force a prior solution into the wrong client context.

Proof

Dependencies, adaptation boundaries, failure modes, owner, version, and evidence from real use.

Decision architecture

Make the hard calls earlier.

The operating model matters only if it improves decisions—not paperwork.

01

Client-specific or reusable?

Separate context from mechanism before an engagement-specific solution becomes misleading IP.

02

AI, deterministic service, or human authority?

Place each responsibility where it can be bounded, explained, and operated safely.

03

Release, hold, or stop?

Use evidence thresholds for quality, privacy, security, latency, cost, and adoption.

04

Escalate or absorb?

Make cross-practice ownership and interface risk explicit before schedule pressure hides it.

05

Harvest or retire?

Promote only patterns that carry real-use evidence, dependencies, and known failure modes.

Hard-objection analysis

“The posting asks for 2-5 years of Azure AI delivery. You do not have that exact tenure.”

Correct. I will not market adjacency as equivalence. The risk is Azure-specific delivery repetition, and it should be tested directly.

What transfers immediately is the work beneath the tool names: agentic workflow patterns, retrieval and human-in-the-loop design, evaluation and governance rails, cross-system integration, high-reliability quality mechanisms, technical-business translation, and operating systems that make delivery visible.

Judge the risk with bounded technical casework and an architecture-to-evaluation exercise. I would enter with humility on platform specifics and urgency in closing them.

Executive interview questions

Questions that expose the real seams.

Where do AI engagements most often lose time today: architecture ambiguity, data readiness, cross-practice handoffs, evaluation, security review, or adoption?
What distinguishes a technically strong Senior Consultant from one who materially improves 3Cloud’s delivery system?
How does MILO decide what becomes reusable IP versus what remains engagement-specific?
Post-acquisition, where should 3Cloud’s delivery identity remain distinctive, and where should Cognizant scale change the model?
What evidence earns trust when a consultant coordinates across Data & AI, App Innovation, and Cloud Platform?
What would make you say after six months: “this person reduced delivery risk and made the people around them better”?
