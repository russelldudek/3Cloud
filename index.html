Operating artifact

The Delivery Weave makes integration testable.

Three technical threads pass through five delivery gates. The point is not ceremony. Each gate makes a different decision explicit before hidden assumptions become rework.

01

Ground the work before the architecture hardens.

Decision

What workflow outcome matters, what data boundary applies, and where human authority must remain?

Failure mode

A technically impressive prototype with unclear user behavior, ownership, evidence, or acceptance criteria.

Proof

Baseline, workflow map, data contract, risk assumptions, and a small evaluation set created before build speed becomes sunk cost.

02

Compose the smallest coherent system.

Decision

Which responsibilities belong in retrieval, tools, orchestration, deterministic services, UI, and human review?

Failure mode

One giant prompt or agent absorbs business rules, security boundaries, and integration logic that should be explicit.

Proof

Traceable architecture decisions, interface contracts, bounded tool permissions, fallback paths, and cost assumptions.

03

Prove behavior, not demo charisma.

Decision

What does “good” mean for task success, groundedness, safety, privacy, latency, and cost?

Failure mode

Stakeholders approve a demo while edge cases, hallucination paths, threat surfaces, and escalation failures remain invisible.

Proof

Golden cases, adversarial cases, human review criteria, failure taxonomy, and explicit release thresholds.

04

Observe the system in the work.

Decision

Which telemetry separates usage from value and distinguishes model issues from workflow, data, or integration issues?

Failure mode

Teams count calls and tokens while users route around the system or quality degradation goes unnoticed.

Proof

Outcome metrics, adoption depth, override/escalation rates, defect signals, latency, unit economics, and review cadence.

05

Harvest what is reusable without flattening client context.

Decision

What should become a component, accelerator, framework, playbook, evaluation asset, or cautionary pattern?

Failure mode

Teams either rebuild everything or force a prior solution into the wrong context because reuse was not qualified.

Proof

Clear dependency notes, adaptation boundaries, known failure modes, owner, versioning, and evidence from real use.

Proposed scorecard · hypothesis for discovery

Measure the seams, not just the sprint.

The exact targets belong to 3Cloud and the client. The structure below is how I would test whether speed is becoming durable value rather than accelerated rework.

01 DELIVERY

Architecture-to-accepted increment

Cycle time, milestone predictability, escaped defects, and rework caused by integration assumptions.

02 AI QUALITY

Task success under stress

Groundedness, safety, adversarial cases, human review agreement, and failure-mode coverage.

03 ECONOMICS

Value per unit of delivery

Utilization context, cloud/LLM unit cost, avoided rework, and the cost of quality controls.

04 ADOPTION

Behavior after launch

Workflow penetration, repeat use, time saved where verified, overrides, escalations, and abandonment signals.

05 TRUST

Governance that moves with delivery

Review coverage, decision traceability, permission boundaries, privacy controls, and audit readiness.

06 REUSE

Learning that compounds

Pattern adoption, harvest-to-reuse latency, adaptation burden, version health, and downstream delivery lift.

90-day entry plan

Earn trust through bounded delivery, then compound it.

DAYS 0-30

Learn the house style.

  • Map 3Cloud architecture, delivery, review, and MILO patterns.
  • Pair with senior technical leaders on active design reviews.
  • Close Azure-specific knowledge gaps through the exact stack used by the team.
  • Build a seam-risk map from one active engagement.

DAYS 31-60

  • Translate architecture into a small delivery plan with acceptance criteria.
  • Stand up evaluation and telemetry before release pressure peaks.

  • Mentor through pairing and review, not abstract advice.

  • Run a closeout on what was client-specific versus reusable.

  • Propose the next 90-day technical depth and delivery leverage plan.

Exit evidence: repeatable asset candidate, validated learning, and a clear next-level ownership path.

Red-team memo

The obvious objection

“This role asks for 2-5 years of Azure AI delivery. Your verified evidence is broader AI and operating transformation, not a multi-year Azure delivery record.”

My answer

Correct. I would not market adjacency as equivalence. The risk in my candidacy is Azure-specific delivery reps, and it should be tested directly.

What transfers immediately is the work beneath the tool names: agentic workflow patterns, retrieval and human-in-the-loop design, evaluation and governance rails, cross-system integration, high-reliability quality mechanisms, client-facing technical translation, and delivery operating systems.

My proposal is simple: judge me on a bounded technical case and architecture-to-evaluation exercise. I would enter with humility on the platform specifics and confidence in the integration, quality, and operating disciplines that make AI survive contact with real work.
