3Cloud's Senior Consultant - AI role sits in the part of enterprise AI delivery where good ideas most often become expensive: the seams between architecture and implementation, data and applications, model behavior and human work, project speed and evidence of quality.

That problem is especially interesting now. 3Cloud entered 2026 as part of Cognizant, expanding the scale available behind its Azure, Data & AI, and App Innovation capabilities. At the same time, MILO is explicitly turning delivery learning into agents, accelerators, components, and frameworks. To me, the role's real mandate is to help architecture survive contact with delivery - and help delivery learning compound.

My career has been built around that kind of integration. At Vape-Jet, I lead AI-first operating transformation across production, quality, customer success, support, engineering issue flow, ERP/Odoo, Jira, HubSpot, Slack, telemetry, and AI-assisted workflows. Through DudeWorth, I develop AI-readiness and agentic workflow patterns that connect retrieval, agents, human judgment, escalation, evaluation, governance, adoption burden, reuse, and time-to-value. At Amazon, I built operating mechanisms for a 24/7 customer-backward environment. At Compunetics, I led engineering, manufacturing, quality, and technical delivery in high-reliability settings.

I also want to address the obvious concern directly. The posting asks for 2-5 years of Azure delivery experience. My verified record is broader AI and operating transformation experience, not a multi-year Azure consulting tenure, and I will not pretend otherwise. What I bring is the adjacent discipline that often determines whether AI delivery works: decomposing complex workflows, designing human authority and escalation, making quality measurable, connecting technical and business teams, and turning repeated execution into standard work and reusable mechanisms.

My candidate vision proposes a simple operating model: AI delivery scales at the seams. Ground the workflow and data boundary. Compose the smallest coherent system. Prove behavior with evaluation and risk evidence. Observe the system in real work. Harvest what is genuinely reusable. The model is not a claim about 3Cloud's undisclosed process; it is a concrete way to show how I think about the mandate.

I would welcome a technical case or architecture-to-evaluation exercise that tests the risk in my candidacy directly. I would enter with humility about platform-specific gaps, urgency in closing them, and confidence in the integration, quality, delivery, and adoption disciplines I have built across my career.

Carpe diem,
